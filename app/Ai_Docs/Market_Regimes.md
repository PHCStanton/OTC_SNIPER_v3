# MARKET REGIMES IN OTC BIMARY OPTIONS

1. Trending Market Structures

Before trading, you must identify the current market structure. Let's start with trending conditions.

Trending with Pullbacks

Price makes clear higher highs and higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs and lower lows (downtrend) with regular pullbacks. Moderate momentum, not parabolic.

Ideal for Bollinger Band pullback strategies

Enter with trend when price touches opposite band

2. Strong Momentum Trending

Price moves in one direction with minimal pullbacks, often walking along one Bollinger Band. Large, consistent candles.

Difficult to trade with most strategies

Wait for momentum to cool before entering

3. Ranging or Sideways

Price bounces between clear support and resistance with no overall direction. Suits mean reversion strategies and oscillator-based entries.

4. Choppy or Messy

Lots of wicks, fake breakouts, unpredictable reversals with no clear structure. Reduce position size or avoid trading entirely.

5. Breakout Conditions

Price breaks through significant support, resistance, or range boundary. Requires confirmation with follow-through and increased volatility.

6. Trend Reversal

Overall direction changes—uptrend becomes downtrend or vice versa. Involves breaking key levels and changing pattern of highs and lows.
